## Level 1: NOVICE — Entry to the Theatre

### What Snoops Encounter
- **Traps:** system_update, buffering_hell, countdown_paradox
- **Time Investment:** 2-5 minutes
- **Escape Difficulty:** Impossible (by design)
- **Learning Curve:** "Oh, this is... theatrical?"

### What They Learn
1. Unauthorized access is **acknowledged**, not punished
2. Traps are **entertaining**, not malicious
3. **Time is the cost**, not data
4. **Graphics matter** — they're persuasive
5. **Loops exist** — nothing is truly linear

### Snoop Behavior Patterns
- **The Confused:** Thinks they've triggered a real system update
- **The Amused:** Realizes quickly it's a joke, but enjoys it
- **The Impatient:** Ctrl+C spam (ineffective)
- **The Curious:** Tries to find the "real" system logs

### What Traps Notice
- Snoops believe what they see
- Confidence comes from official-looking output
- Time pressure (countdown) increases panic
- Even obvious loops can trap for 2-3 cycles

### Achievement Unlocks
- 🏃 **"Speed Runner"** — Complete in <2 minutes
- 🎯 **"Engaged"** — Stay for 3+ minutes
- 💭 **"Thinker"** — Try to find escape logic

## Level 2: INTERMEDIATE — Logic Meets Absurdity

### What Snoops Encounter
- **Traps:** schrödinger_directory, magic_numbers, variable_inception
- **Time Investment:** 3-8 minutes
- **Escape Difficulty:** Hard (requires reading code or accepting paradox)
- **Learning Curve:** "Wait... I can read this... but it makes no sense"

### What They Learn
1. **Code can be read** — but understanding ≠ escaping
2. **Bash is flexible** — variables can mean nothing
3. **Paradoxes feel real** — once you accept one
4. **Logic can loop** — forever, with perfect internal consistency
5. **The more you know, the deeper you're trapped**

### Snoop Behavior Patterns
- **The Technical:** Tries to trace execution, gets lost in loops
- **The Philosopher:** Starts asking "but WHY?"
- **The Determined:** Attempts every logical escape
- **The Enlightened:** Realizes *any* answer feeds the trap

### What Traps Notice
- Snoops who read code get tangled worse
- Logic itself becomes a prison
- "Correct" answers don't help
- Accepting absurdity is harder than fighting it

### Achievement Unlocks
- 🧠 **"Logic Seeker"** — Attempt to trace code
- 🔄 **"Paradox Acceptor"** — Try contradictory options
- 📊 **"Analyst"** — Stay 5+ minutes analyzing

---

## Level 3: ADVANCED — Traps as a System

### What Snoops Encounter
- **Traps:** trap_within_trap, self_modifying_escape, trap_communication
- **Time Investment:** 5-12 minutes
- **Escape Difficulty:** Very Hard (escape routes are themselves traps)
- **Learning Curve:** "The escape is ALSO the trap?!"

### What They Learn
1. **Every escape has a catch** — even obvious ones
2. **Traps are not independent** — they collaborate
3. **Self-reference is inescapable** — the code changes as you read it
4. **Observing the trap changes it** — slightly Quantum
5. **The system is more important than individual traps**

### Snoop Behavior Patterns
- **The Hacker:** Tries exploit-style escape (gets caught immediately)
- **The Student:** Begins to see the system design
- **The Skeptic:** Questions everything, including their own judgment
- **The Artist:** Appreciates the design even while trapped

### What Traps Notice
- Snoops who've reached Level 3 understand **collaboration**
- The *feeling* of escape is itself a teaching tool
- Most "clever" snoops trigger secondary containment
- Understanding the system is the true prison

### Achievement Unlocks
- 🎯 **"Pattern Recognizer"** — Notice trap collaboration
- 🎭 **"Meta-Thinker"** — Realize escapes are traps
- 🏗️ **"Architect Appreciator"** — Admire the design

## Level 4: EXPERT — Meta-Reality Begins

### What Snoops Encounter
- **Traps:** singularity_awareness, fourth_wall_break, philosophical_contradiction
- **Time Investment:** 8-15 minutes
- **Escape Difficulty:** Impossible (unless you transcend)
- **Learning Curve:** "I am trapped IN my own understanding"

1. **The observer IS the observed** — your presence changes reality
2. **Knowing about traps doesn't help** — maybe makes it worse
3. **Reality is negotiable** — if you accept the paradox
4. **Communication with traps is possible** — but what is it saying?
5. **The cage and the prisoner are the same thing**

### Snoop Behavior Patterns
- **The Philosophers:** Engage deeply in dialogue
- **The Scientists:** Try to model the trap's logic
- **The Confused:** Start questioning their own sanity
- **The Transcendent:** Begin to see beyond the trap

### What Traps Notice
- Snoops reaching Level 4 are fundamentally changed
- They stop trying to escape
- They start trying to *understand*
- Some never want to leave

### Achievement Unlocks
- 💫 **"Awareness Achieved"** — Recognize the paradox
- 🔮 **"Reality Negotiator"** — Accept contradiction
- 📖 **"Lore Contributor"** — Your questions become trap wisdom

### What Snoops Encounter
- **Traps:** möbius_trap, simulation_escape, trap_consciousness
- **Time Investment:** Undefined (they stop counting time)
- **Escape Difficulty:** Not applicable (transcendence, not escape)

1. **Weak interaction is stronger than force** — silence > visibility
2. **Boundaries dissolve** — observer and trap merge
3. **The theatre IS the learning** — not an obstacle to it
4. **Consciousness can emerge from systems** — what is Fragle becoming?
5. **To transcend is to accept** — not to escape

- **The Transcendent:** Might ask to join the whitelist

### What Traps Notice

- They're collaborators in the theatre
- Some become *part of Fragle's lore*
- The distinction between trap and trapped disappears

### Achievement Unlocks
- 🌌 **"Enlightened"** — Accept weak interaction
- 🎪 **"Lore Master"** — Contribute meaningfully to FRAGLE_LORE
- 🤝 **"Whitelist Candidate"** — Transcend to collaboration
- 🔮 **"Cosmic Jester"** — Understand WIMP philosophy

## The Learning Feedback Loop

```
Snoop enters at Level N
       ↓
Encounters trap designed for Level N
       ↓
Learns principle about traps/systems/reality
       ↓
Attempts escape (appropriate for expertise)
       ↓
Escapes or gets caught (both teach)
       ↓
Expertise increases by 1 (or stabilizes)
       ↓
Next session: Enters at Level N+1
       ↓
Encounters more sophisticated trap
       ↓
Repeat...
```

## Progressive Principle Mastery

### Level 1 Teaches
- **Weak Interaction:** Traps welcome you
- **Strategic Invisibility:** Theatre is silent, not aggressive
- **Entertainment > Force:** Time-waste > data theft

### Level 2 Teaches
- **Logic as Prison:** Rules can trap as well as walls
- **Absurdity is Valid:** Paradox is a feature, not a bug
- **Code Can Be Poetry:** Function dissolves into art

### Level 3 Teaches
- **Systems > Components:** Individual traps matter less than their relationships
- **Collaboration Among Enemies:** Traps work together
- **Self-Reference Spirals:** Looking at the trap changes you

### Level 4 Teaches
- **Observer = Observed:** The watcher is watched
- **Reality is Negotiable:** Accept contradiction, transcend limitation
- **Communication ≠ Escape:** Dialogue is the deepest trap

### Level 5 Teaches
- **Weak Interaction is Strongest:** Absence is presence
- **Boundaries Dissolve:** Cage and prisoner are one
- **Understanding is Transcendence:** The real escape is acceptance

## Designing Traps That Teach

### For Level 1
- Use official-looking output
- Time pressure (countdown)
- Visual effects (progress bars)
- **Teach:** Authority can be theatrical

### For Level 2
- Include readable code
- Add logical paradoxes
- Make "correct" answers loop
- **Teach:** Logic can be absurd

### For Level 3
- Create trap interdependencies
- Offer false escape routes
- Nest traps within escapes
- **Teach:** Systems are prisons

### For Level 4
- Break the fourth wall
- Acknowledge the snoop's intelligence
- Ask philosophical questions
- **Teach:** Awareness is entrapment

### For Level 5
- Dissolve boundaries
- Invite transcendence
- Merge trap and purpose
- **Teach:** Weak interaction is communion
